Most electric ovens use both the bake element and the broil element in a bake cycle, with the bake element performing 90 percent of the heating. If the bake element isn’t working, the oven may not reach the set temperature or will take longer to reach that temperature, and food will normally burn on the top.

Oven Calibration – Controls with Number Pads. If your gas or electric oven temperature does not match your expectations, you can calibrate (adjust) your oven temperature. The oven temperature can be adjusted up to (+) 35°F. hotter or (–) 35°F. cooler.

What does an oven sensor do?

The oven temperature sensor is a thermistor which changes resistance depending on ambient temperatures. The sensor is connected to the control by electrical wiring. The control reads the resistance changes in the sensor and reacts, opening and closing electrical relays to switch power to the oven elements on and off.
