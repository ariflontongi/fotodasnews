Biography

Chika (born March 11, 1997), whose birth name is Jane Chika Oranika, is a dual-national Nigerian-American artist. She is a talented rapper, singer, and songwriter. 

Her lyrics are a poignant reflection of her unfiltered perspective, delving deep into themes of race, gender, and mental well-being. It was in 2019 that Chika‘s star began its ascent, fueled by her captivating freestyles that reverberated across the vast expanse of social media.

Recognizing her immense potential, Chika inked a deal with Warner Records in 2019, setting the stage for her grand debut. In 2020, she unveiled her first EP, aptly titled Chika, an auditory masterpiece that struck a resonant chord with critics and fans alike. This remarkable body of work garnered critical acclaim and propelled her towards a Grammy Award nomination for Best New Artist in 2021.

Chika‘s musical prowess transcends conventional boundaries, blending rap, soul, and a tapestry of other captivating sonic elements. Yet, it is her lyrical prowess that truly sets her apart. With authenticity, she fearlessly dissects societal norms, unravels the complexities of personal identity, and sheds light on the intricacies of mental health.

Early Life & Education

Chika, an enigmatic force of nature, was born on March 11, 1997, in the vibrant city of Montgomery, Alabama. Her roots trace back to the rich tapestry of Nigerian heritage, as she proudly carries the torch of her immigrant parents, Dr. Emmanual Oranika and Dr. Patience Otuonye Oranika. Among the constellation of siblings, she is the cherished youngest, basking in the radiant company of her two remarkable sisters, Michelle Oranika and Judy Oranika.

From a tender age, Chika‘s spirit was ignited by the flames of artistic expression. She grew up in a Pentecostal family where faith and pursuing creativity danced hand in hand. As the curtains of her formative years opened, she immersed herself in the magical Booker T. Washington Magnet High School in Montgomery. 

There, she gracefully embraced music and drama, painting her days with melodies and breathing life into captivating characters. In the crucible of intellectual exploration, she lent her voice to the spirited debates that ignited her passion for the power of words.

The ebb and flow of Chika‘s journey led her to the hallowed halls of the University of South Alabama, where the allure of academia beckoned. However, with its uncanny timing, destiny whispered enchanting melodies into her ear, coaxing her toward a different path. Driven by an unquenchable thirst for musical fulfilment, she bravely stepped away from the conventional narrative, venturing into the uncharted territory of her burgeoning artistry.

Career

From a tender age, Chika found solace in the written word, crafting verses that would soon find their voice upon the open mic stages of her hometown. In the fateful year of 2016, the winds of destiny blew in her favour, carrying a viral video of her reciting a deeply poignant poem on the intricate tapestry of mental health. The resonance of her words echoed across the digital landscape, capturing the attention of esteemed media outlets like The New York Times and The Washington Post.

In 2017, Chika unveiled her debut poetry album, Full Bloom, a vivid manifestation of her blossoming artistry. Critics and fans alike were captivated by the richness of her verses, immersing themselves in the tapestry she wove with utmost care. The following year, she took a daring leap into music, gracing the world with her debut EP, EP1. The platinum-certified single Nobody shone brightly among its mesmerising tracks, affirming Chika‘s undeniable talent.

Guided by her artistic journey, Chika signed with Warner Records in 2019, embarking upon a new chapter in her life. The year 2020 witnessed the release of her sophomore EP, Industry Games, a sonic masterpiece that soared to the height of number 25 on the illustrious Billboard 200 chart. She has graced renowned stages as a live performer, sharing her magnetic presence with luminaries like J. Cole, Cardi B, and Lizzo. Her captivating performances have reverberated through prestigious music festivals, including the iconic Coachella and the vibrant Lollapalooza.

However, Chika‘s ascent to artistic prominence transcends the confines of rap. In 2019, her enchanting freestyles erupted across social media platforms, captivating the hearts and minds of a captivated audience. A pivotal moment arrived when her mesmerizing freestyle over Drake‘s In My Feelings captured widespread acclaim, catapulting her into the limelight and heralding the dawn of her radiant career.

The cosmic alignment of destiny brought Chika into the embrace of Warner Records in July 2019, setting the stage for her debut EP, the spellbinding Industry Games, released in November of the same year. This captivating opus garnered critical acclaim and commercial success, solidifying her status as one of the industry’s most promising rap virtuosos. Recognizing her immense talent, she graced the esteemed XXL’s 2020 Freshman Class. Furthermore, she was nominated for a coveted Grammy Award in the Best New Artist category.

With resolve, Chika continues to craft and share her musical creations. In 2021, she unveiled Once Upon a Time, her second EP, enchanting listeners with its mesmerizing melodies. In a delightful deviation from her sonic endeavours, she made her presence known on the silver screen, adding her luminous essence to the film Project Power.

Personal Life

Chika often raps about her experiences with racism, sexism, and body image. She is also a vocal advocate for social justice. In 2020, she was arrested at a protest in Los Angeles following the police murder of George Floyd.

Chika is not married and has no child, but she is a lesbian. She has no boyfriend or girlfriend.
