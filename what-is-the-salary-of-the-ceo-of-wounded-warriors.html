How does the Wounded Warrior Project spend its donations?

The Wounded Warrior Project allocates its donations to several programs aimed at supporting veterans and their families. The primary programs that it offers include:

  • The Long-Term Support Trust, which handles the needs of veterans who were seriously injured during combat
  • The Warrior Care Network, which provides mental health therapies to post-9/11 veterans who suffer from post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • The Independence Program, which assists veterans with mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI) or PTSD and their families in improving their quality of life

What is the Wounded Warrior Project’s overhead rate?

The Wounded Warrior Project’s overhead rate, which is often referred to as the “cost to raise a dollar,” is around 19.8%. This rate implies that for every dollar raised by the Wounded Warrior Project, about $0.20 is spent on administrative expenses, management, and fundraising.
