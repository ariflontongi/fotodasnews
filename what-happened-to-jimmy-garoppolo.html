Early Career and Rise to Prominence

Jimmy Garoppolo’s journey in the NFL began when he was drafted by the New England Patriots in the second round of the 2014 NFL Draft. Serving as a backup to Tom Brady, Garoppolo’s opportunities were limited, but he showed promise in the appearances he made. His big break came in 2017 when he was traded to the San Francisco 49ers.

Upon joining the 49ers, Garoppolo quickly became a fan favorite, leading the team to five straight wins to close out the 2017 season. His performance earned him a lucrative contract extension, making him one of the highest-paid quarterbacks at the time.

  • Ended the 2017 season with a 5-0 record as a starter

Injury Setbacks

Garoppolo’s momentum was halted by a series of injuries. In 2018, he suffered a torn ACL in Week 3, ending his season prematurely. He returned in 2019 to lead the 49ers to a Super Bowl appearance, but injuries continued to plague him in subsequent seasons.

  • Torn ACL in 2018

  • Missed 10 games in the 2020 season due to injuries

Super Bowl LIV Appearance

In the 2019 season, Garoppolo led the 49ers to a 13-3 record and a Super Bowl LIV appearance. Although the 49ers lost to the Kansas City Chiefs, Garoppolo’s role in getting the team to the championship game solidified his status as a capable starting quarterback.

  • Threw for 3,978 yards and 27 touchdowns in the 2019 regular season
