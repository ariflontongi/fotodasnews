Career

Guccio opened his shop in Florence, which started a fashion empire that would become known worldwide. Initially, the shop sold imported leather goods, but as Guccio’s sons joined the company, they began creating high-quality leather products. 

The brand’s popularity continued to grow, and in 1933, Aldo Gucci designed the famous double-G logo that is still recognized today. By 1938, the company expanded to Rome, solidifying its position as a prestigious and highly sought-after luxury brand.

Guccio’s emphasis on quality and attention to detail, the company has faced criticism for unethical practices in its supply chain, including the use of forced labor and environmentally harmful production methods. 

These controversies have tarnished the brand’s reputation and raised concerns about its commitment to ethical and sustainable practices. However, the brand remains a leader in the luxury fashion industry, with its iconic designs and enduring legacy. 

After founding the company in 1921, Guccio Gucci focused on creating high-quality leather goods, particularly luggage and handbags. His attention to detail and commitment to exceptional craftsmanship quickly gained popularity among wealthy customers, laying the foundation for the future success of the Gucci fashion house. Gucci is known for its iconic designs, luxurious materials, and global influence in the fashion industry today.
